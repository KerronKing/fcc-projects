Shigeru Miyamoto

One of gaming's greatest minds.

Shigeru Miyamoto - the man behind Mario

• 1952 - Shigeru Miyamoto was born.


• 1977 - Shigeru Miyamoto joins Nintendo as an artist.


• 1980 - He helps the company to develop the game Radar Scope.


• 1981 - 1983 - Shigeru Miyamoto championed the ground-breaking title Donkey Kong.


• 1985 - 1990 - Shigeru Miyamoto was brought the Mario and Zelda series to life. Both of which would end up becoming two of the top selling game franchises of all time.


• 1990 - 2000 - Shigeru Miyamoto headed a merger between Nintendo's research and development teams. He oversaw the development and release of the SNES and N64 game consoles, as well as updated versions of several Nintendo classics. He also mentored Satoshi Tajiri, guiding him during the creation process of Pocket Monsters: Red and Green (released in English as Pokémon Red and Blue), the initial video games in the Pokémon series.


• 2000 - 2011 - Shigeru continued being an integral part of Nintendo's operations into the new millenium, helping develop titles for the GameCube, Wii and Nintendo DS. He also played a major role in the development of the Wii, a console that popularized motion control gaming, and its launch title Wii Sports, which helped show the capability of the new control scheme.


• 2011 - present - Following the death of Nintendo president Satoru Iwata in July 2015, Miyamoto was appointed as an acting Representative Director, alongside Genyo Takeda.
